What is a will?

A will is a document that sets out your instructions for your estate (what you own + what you owe) after you die. In Ontario, your will usually names an executor to carry out your instructions.

Key job
Names an executor
The executor gathers assets, pays debts/taxes, and distributes property.
Key decision
Who inherits
Specific gifts (items/money) + ‚Äúresidue‚Äù (everything left over).
Optional
Guardian for minors
If you have children under 18, this is an important planning step.

What happens if you die without a will?

If you die without a will, you are intestate. Ontario law sets out who inherits and who can apply to manage the estate. This can lead to delays and conflict.

Real-life takeaway: A will can reduce family disputes, prevent confusion, and speed up estate administration. It‚Äôs also a chance to pick someone you trust to handle everything (executor).

Common mistakes (and how to avoid them)

1) Wrong witnesses

Use 2 appropriate witnesses. Avoid beneficiaries/witness conflicts. Use the witness tool above.

2) No backups

Always name an alternate executor and backup beneficiaries where possible.

3) Not updating

Life changes can make old plans unrealistic. Review regularly.

4) Unclear wording

Vague gifts cause disputes. Be specific and consistent.
